Kumbh Mela
The Kumbh Mela is so massive that the crowds are visible from space. In early 2025, the Prayagraj Mahakumbh saw millions of pilgrims gather to bathe in the sacred confluence of the Ganges, Yamuna, and Saraswati rivers to wash away their sins.
Frog Weddings
In states like Assam and Karnataka, when the monsoon is delayed, villagers perform "Mandooka Parinaya" (frog weddings). It is believed that marrying a pair of frogs with full traditional Hindu rituals will appease the rain gods and end a drought.
The Tilak
The colored dot (Bindi) or mark (Tilak) on the forehead isn't just aesthetic. It is placed on the Ajna Chakra (the "third eye"), a point between the eyebrows believed to be the seat of latent energy and concentration.
Snake Boat Racing
In the backwaters of Kerala, the Vallam Kali (Snake Boat Race) features 100-foot-long boats manned by over 100 rowers. It is a rhythmic, high-energy tradition that dates back to the naval wars of the 13th century.
